# Greek Reading Lesson Sequence

This staged outline is a review-ready starting point, not final learner-facing
lesson copy. It uses Modern Greek monotonic spelling and targets practical
traveler reading before full alphabet-chart completion.

## Course Shape

- First-session reading win: read `νερό`, `καφέ`, and `μετρό` as real signs or
  menu words.
- Approximate lesson count: 14 core lessons plus optional late review for rare
  clusters and politeness phrases.
- Structural family: alphabet with position-sensitive final sigma, stress mark,
  vowel mergers, and high-frequency digraphs.
- Required app support beyond the current Thai runtime: language-agnostic text
  fields, final-sigma or positional variant metadata, digraph pedagogical units,
  case-aware display, and per-course script styling.

## Stage 1: First Decoding Wins

| Lesson | Anchor  | New units                          | Rule or pattern                                      | Review units       | Drill focus                                                     |
| ------ | ------- | ---------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1      | `νερό`  | Ν ν, Ε ε, Ρ ρ, Ο ο, τόνος          | Tonos marks the stressed syllable.                   | None               | Match visible letters to sound and identify the stress mark.    |
| 2      | `καφέ`  | Κ κ, Α α, Φ φ                      | Greek φ is modern /f/.                               | Ε ε, τόνος         | Build two-syllable menu words from known units.                 |
| 3      | `μετρό` | Μ μ, Τ τ                           | Greek τ is /t/ and transfer from Latin T is helpful. | Ν ν, Ε ε, Ρ ρ, Ο ο | Read transit/cafe words with repeated CV chunks.                |
| 4      | `ένα`   | final review of Α, Ε, Ν plus tonos | Compact high-frequency words can still carry stress. | Ν ν, Ε ε, Α α      | Recognize short function words without treating them as filler. |

## Stage 2: Core Coverage

| Lesson | Anchor   | New units    | Rule or pattern                                             | Review units         | Drill focus                                                      |
| ------ | -------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5      | `σαλάτα` | Σ σ, Λ λ     | σ is the normal sigma form inside or at the start of words. | Α α, Τ τ             | Identify sigma and decode repeated vowel patterns in menu words. |
| 6      | `οδός`   | Δ δ, final ς | ς appears at the end of a word; σ appears elsewhere.        | Ο ο, Σ σ             | Sort words by medial sigma and final sigma.                      |
| 7      | `στάση`  | Η η          | η is a spelling of the modern /i/ sound.                    | Σ σ, Τ τ, Α α, τόνος | Compare η with previously taught ι-like sounds once introduced.  |
| 8      | `τιμή`   | Ι ι          | ι and η can sound alike while remaining separate spellings. | Τ τ, Μ μ, Η η        | Read price and shopping words with i-sound spelling variants.    |
| 9      | `ταξί`   | Ξ ξ          | ξ is the /ks/ cluster.                                      | Τ τ, Α α, Ι ι, τόνος | Decode a high-utility travel word with one cluster unit.         |
| 10     | `γάλα`   | Γ γ          | γ is a fricative and changes feel before front vowels.      | Α α, Λ λ             | Pronunciation review and gamma contrast drills.                  |

## Stage 3: Variants, Confusables, And Domain Text

| Lesson | Anchor      | New units                      | Rule or pattern                                           | Review units                | Drill focus                                             |
| ------ | ----------- | ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| 11     | `όχι`       | Χ χ                            | χ is a velar or palatal fricative, not English x.         | Ο ο, Ι ι, τόνος             | Read yes/no and warning-context words with χ.           |
| 12     | `είσοδος`   | ει digraph                     | ει is a common spelling for the /i/ sound.                | Σ σ/ς, Δ δ, Ο ο             | Pair entrance/exit signs and final-sigma recognition.   |
| 13     | `έξοδος`    | Ε with tonos review, Ξ ξ reuse | Entrances and exits share a visual word family.           | ει, Σ σ/ς, Δ δ              | Contrast `είσοδος` and `έξοδος` as environmental print. |
| 14     | `κοτόπουλο` | Π π, Υ υ, ου digraph           | ου is /u/; υ alone usually joins the /i/ spelling family. | Κ κ, Ο ο, Τ τ, Λ λ          | Read a longer menu anchor by chunking known units.      |
| 15     | `μουσείο`   | ου and ει consolidation        | Multiple digraphs can appear in one high-utility word.    | Μ μ, Σ σ, Ο ο               | Decode cultural-site words and review vowel mergers.    |
| 16     | `φαρμακείο` | Long facility-word chunks      | Public signs can be decoded by chunking familiar units.   | Φ φ, Α α, Ρ ρ, Μ μ, Κ κ, ει | Read public-facility words with long familiar chunks.   |

| ------ | ----------- | ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| 17     | `ψωμί`      | Ψ ψ, Ω ω                  | Rare clusters can wait until a strong menu anchor justifies them.       | Μ μ, Ι ι, τόνος                   | Decode a late food word with two high-load units.         |
| 18     | `προσοχή`   | Safety-word consolidation | Safety words justify higher new-load after chi and final eta are known. | Π π, Ρ ρ, Ο ο, Σ σ, Χ χ, Η η      | Read warning signs and choose correct meaning.            |

| 21     | `ευχαριστώ` | ευ digraph                | ευ can sound /ev/ or /ef/ depending on the next sound.                  | Χ χ, Α α, Ρ ρ, Ι ι, Σ σ, Τ τ, Ω ω | Treat as a late heart word and pronunciation review item. |

## Coverage Notes

- Target-domain samples: menus, cafe boards, street signs, transit labels,
  public-facility labels, pharmacy signs, museum/place labels, and open/closed
  storefront signs.
- Known-grapheme or known-unit coverage goal: by Lesson 10, cover the highest
  payoff base letters needed for short menu and transit words; by Lesson 16,
  cover final sigma, tonos, eta/iota spelling contrast, xi, chi, and the `ει` and
  `ου` digraphs.
- Known-word or known-anchor coverage goal: early learner should recognize at
  least five immediately useful words by Lesson 4 and a compact sign/menu set by
  Lesson 12.
- Deferred high-load material: diaeresis, polytonic marks, full ancient Greek
  pronunciation, rare psi-first words, the full consonant-digraph inventory,
  detailed inflection, and production spelling.
